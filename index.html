abaca tabi is an attempt to seamlessly bring order from the chaos that is a large restaurant dining experience. Real life is never black and white. While the more the merrier is generally true, in a restaurant or bar, each dining partner multiplies the complexity of who is going to pay for how much of what. After a few festive beverages, it can be even harder to determine the finer details of local taxes and included service fees. Ideally some generous patriarchal figure is around to claim the check and pay a large lump sum. abaca tabi is for all the other times, when that guy is not around, and a herd of cats wants to go home without leaving someone behind to wash dishes all night. So start at the beginning, how much is the merchant expecting and how do we get there?

After providing a subtotal and tax estimate as initial parameters, an adjustable tip is added in and the user is given an empty table to fill with payers and items from the receipt. The OVERVIEW tab keeps track of the remainder. This is how much of the bill has not been filled in by the user with items from the check. When this remainder is zero, all the data elements needed to calculate individual responsibilities have been documented.

Adding an item into abaca tabi only requires a name and price.

Once an item is added, the user can do a long press to open the share item dialog, potentially dividing up a large item into pieces for each payer to cover.

Each payer tab allows for the addition of items and displays individual subtotals, taxes, tips, and grand totals.

While the default name of each payer is Payer N, this can be cumbersome when trying to remember who is who when sharing items. A long press on the payer tab allows access to payer properties. An email address can be entered here, or one from the user address book can be used later.

Finally when everything is ready, the user has precise answers for all parties involved. If verbal communication and proximities are an issue, the user can email each payer a record of the future transaction that is about to be processed.

FAQ

What does 'abaca tabi' mean?
The name is intended to provoke more than a few thoughts. An abacus is a counting frame. A calculating tool. A tab can be a bill or check for a meal in a restaurant. A cost of something. A space between columns of data. Old machines that read, sort, and print information from punched cards were called tabulators. A Tabi is also a traditional Japanese sock. A glove for your foot to go outside. To keep tabs on something is to carefully observe. And finally, 'abaca tabi' also sounds related to 'abracadabra'. Magic words.
Why would I bother using 'abaca tabi'?
In an age when everyone has a smart phone and credit cards are accepted everywhere, abaca tabi just makes it easier to fairly compensate and bill responible parties.
Isn't 'abaca tabi' just a tip calculator?
Many so called tip calculators do not acknowledge the existance of alcohol and service taxes that can potentially influence the determination of a proper tip. From the start, abaca tabi has also been designed to help groups of people share items and divide up expenses that are not usually calculated on a receipt by the merchant.
